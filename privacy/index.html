Privacy Policy

Solitaire for iOS · Last updated: June 30, 2026

1. Scope

This Privacy Policy explains how the Solitaire iOS app (the “App”) handles information. It does not apply to websites, services, or ads that are operated by third parties, except where this policy describes those third-party services.

2. Information the App stores locally

The App stores gameplay information on your device so the game can function properly. This may include:

  • saved game state and progress;
  • scores, moves, completion times, streaks, and records;
  • sound, haptic, and gameplay preferences; and
  • the installed app version shown in iOS Settings.

This local gameplay information is not sent to the developer’s servers. You can remove it by deleting the App from your device or clearing the App’s data through iOS if that option is available.

3. Advertising and third-party processing

The App uses Google Mobile Ads (AdMob) to show rewarded ads. Google Mobile Ads may process information such as device identifiers, advertising data, coarse location, product interaction data, crash data, performance data, and other diagnostic data for purposes including ad delivery, advertising measurement, analytics, fraud prevention, security, and service improvement.

The App may ask for permission to track you using Apple’s App Tracking Transparency prompt. If you allow tracking, Google may use your device’s advertising identifier to provide or measure personalized advertising. If you do not allow tracking, the App can still show ads, but they may be less personalized.

Third-party service providers that access user data are required to protect that data in accordance with their own privacy commitments, applicable law, and the protections described in this policy. Google’s handling of advertising data is governed by Google’s policies, including the Google Privacy Policy and Google’s advertising technologies policy.

4. Information the developer does not directly collect

The App does not require you to create an account and does not ask you to provide your name, email address, contacts, photos, precise location, or payment information. The developer does not directly sell your personal information.

5. Your choices

  • You can allow or deny tracking when iOS shows the App Tracking Transparency prompt.
  • You can later change tracking permission in iOS Settings > Privacy & Security > Tracking.
  • You can reset or limit advertising personalization using the privacy controls provided by Apple and Google where available.
  • You can delete locally stored game data by deleting the App from your device.

6. Children’s privacy

The App is not directed to children under 13. The developer does not knowingly collect personal information directly from children. If you believe a child has provided personal information through the App, please contact the developer using the contact options below.

7. Data security and retention

Local gameplay data remains on your device until you delete it or uninstall the App. Data processed by Google Mobile Ads is retained and protected according to Google’s policies and service terms.

8. International processing

Google and other service providers may process information in countries other than where you live. Those countries may have different data protection laws than your country or region.

9. Changes to this policy

This policy may be updated from time to time. The “Last updated” date at the top of this page shows when the latest changes were made. Continued use of the App after an update means the updated policy applies.

10. Contact

For privacy questions, use the support contact published with the Solitaire App Store listing. If the project issue tracker is publicly available, you may also contact the developer through GitHub Issues.
